About US Disease Tracker

The US Disease Tracker (USDT) project aims to provide consolidated, epidemiologically sound data, analytics, and insights for monitoring and responding to disease threats across the United States. By partnering with state and local health departments, USDT collects standardized aggregate surveillance data for infectious diseases—such as measles, pertussis, and invasive meningococcal disease—and makes those data accessible through a unified dashboard and open data portal. The project is committed to voluntary participation, robust data standards, and full transparency: only data supplied by jurisdictions are published, no individual-level records are collected, and all submitted data are protected in accordance with each jurisdiction's policies, regulations, and laws. Once the current pilot phase is complete, the dashboard and reported data are intended to become publicly available, enabling researchers, clinicians, and public-health officials to track disease trends in real time and respond swiftly to emerging threats.
